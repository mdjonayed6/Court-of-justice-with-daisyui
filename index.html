What is the Court of Justice?

The Court of Justice is the judicial institution of the European Union (EU). It ensures that EU law is interpreted and applied uniformly across all EU member states.

Who can bring a case before the Court of Justice?

Various parties can bring cases before the Court of Justice, including EU member states, EU institutions, businesses, and individuals.

How does the Court of Justice differ from national courts?

How does the Court of Justice reach decisions?

The Court of Justice consists of judges appointed by each EU member state. Decisions are reached through a deliberative process, where judges analyze legal arguments, review evidence, and apply relevant provisions of EU law.
